I am super excited to articulate my learning from the last 12 months of entrepreneurial hustle. Being a first-time founder, there has been a lot of misconceptions which eventually turns out to be wrong. It is true that the freedom of nobody being bossy around is delightful but on the flip side, it is really scary to be your own boss and you should be best at it to not to lose in the long run.

Like every first time founder, I was also super excited to do something that matters and solve a real problem and bring value to the customer's business and life. Apparently, there is no such framework to build a sustainable startup but there are a few things first time entrepreneurs should really think about at least at the beginning of the hustle to stay in the game.

## The idea is everything. The usual suspect!

Often times, founders believe that the idea is everything and they spent most of their time shaping up the idea and the product. And they truly believe that the customers will come along and buy because the product is super cool! Reality is, nobody wants your cool product unless you bring value to their business and life. Why on earth someone would buy something which doesn't do any good to them?

> "The value of an idea lies in the using of it." - Thomas Edison

You should be brutally focused on the problem statement rather than the product features to build your super cool startup. If you are unclear about the problem you are solving, chances are high that you will go out of your business soon. More often we forget that the idea will not build the business its own, you need feedbacks as early as possible from your prospective customers. And it should be clear whether if you are solving a real problem and people will pay for your solution.

## Vision: The unwelcomed guest

Most of the times, this is really confusing and founders do not want to talk about it unless it is necessary for a presentation. Founders keep themselves busy with the product all the time, and these questions are often exasperating. Having clarity on things you do make you more aligned and you will make less expensive mistakes. And you must remember, it is impossible to solve all the problems in the world by yourself. Hence, you must understand, everybody is not your customer.
The underpinning of a startup is to focus on the problem you solve and the market and customer you serve. And you must have the vision which will help you focus on the right thing or you will end up serving the wrong customers in the long run.

## Someone should stay positive and lead the way

Having clarity on the purpose of existence of the organization is the most important fact every entrepreneur should be thinking all the time. Having said that, there must be someone who is leading the way where the startup wants to go. And he is accountable for taking decisions and building the organization.
Every startup needs a leader who can lead the way or it will ramble around. If all the founders are busy with shaping up the product, then the startup is not going anywhere.
Distractions follow almost all the time, and you need clarity on what you are doing and where you are going. If the leader fails to inspire people and make them believe in himself and the startup, then the aftermath is not always positive.

## You must have that hunger, that killer instinct!

Nobody doesn't really care about you and your startup, but you do. You must have that ruthless determination to succeed, and that must come from within. Remember, If you don't believe in your business idea, then don't expect someone else to believe.

> "Starting your own business is like riding a roller coaster. There are highs and lows and every turn you take is another twist. The lows are really low, but the highs can be really high. You have to be strong, keep your stomach tight, and ride along with the roller coaster that you started."
 - Lindsay Manseau

Be a ruthless believer, fail fast and move forward. Better days are just ahead!
